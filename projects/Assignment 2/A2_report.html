Subtheme: Corporate Landlords & Evictions

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. How much of Boston's housing is owned by major corporations?
    I wanted to first ask this question because this is what the dataset is primarily showing and also to get a feel for how much of Boston’s housing is owned by large corporations. As the reading Housing Justice Beyond the Emergency (Huntley et al., 2022) mentioned, corporate landowners tend to have higher eviction rates.
  2. How has corporate ownership changed over time?
  3. What are the most corporate owned neighborhoods in Boston? Which are the least?
  4. Is corporate property more likely to be vacant?
    As said by the readings evictions are a major issue across the US and also in Boston so this question is to tie vacancy rates to corporate ownership and reveal trends.
  5. What are the demographics like in these highly vacant areas?
    As stated in the readings, Black, Brown, and Indigenous renters are more likely to be evicted than white renters so I wanted to see what the demographics differ in high corporate owned areas and low.

Summary

In conclusion, the corporate ownership and vacancy rates vary greatly across different parts of Boston. In this analysis we explored potential correlations with corporate housing such as vacancy rates and demographic composition. Vacancy rates and corporate ownership seem to be linked mostly at the high and low ends of the values. Meanwhile more moderate values have less of a correlation. From the select neighborhoods there does not seem to be an apparent link between corporate ownership and select demographics. However, this does not mean there is no link whatsoever. The data was not available for other years so it is possible that there were changes in demographics and vacancy rates overtime that were not shown.
